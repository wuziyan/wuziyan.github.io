Ziyan Wu

I am a VP of Engineering at United Imaging Intelligence (UII America) in Boston, MA, where I lead the research and develop of computer vision, machine learning and intelligent robotic solutions for medical environments. Before joining UII I have worked at Siemens Corporate Research in Princeton, NJ and Honeywell Technology Solutions Labs in Shanghai, China respectively.

I received my PhD in Computer and Systems Engineering from Department of Electrical, Computer, and Systems Engineering of Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute in May 2014 under the guidance of Prof. Richard J. Radke.

Research

My research interests are computer vision and machine learning, with special focus on human pose and shape estimation, explainable AI, object detection and tracking, anomaly detection, augmented reality, scene understanding, human re-identification and camera calibration.

Recent Publications

CHROME: Clothed Human Reconstruction with Occlusion-Resilience and Multiview-Consistency from a Single Image

Arindam Dutta, Meng Zheng, Zhongpai Gao, Benjamin Planche, Anwesa Choudhuri, Amit K. Roy-Chowdhury, Terrence Chen, Ziyan Wu

IEEE/CVF International Conference on Computer Vision (ICCV), 2025 (spotlight)

We propose a novel pipeline designed to reconstruct occlusion-resilient 3D humans with multiview consistency from a single occluded image, without requiring either ground-truth geometric prior annotations or 3D supervision.

7DGS: Unified Spatial-Temporal-Angular Gaussian Splatting

Zhongpai Gao, Benjamin Planche, Meng Zheng, Anwesa Choudhuri, Terrence Chen, Ziyan Wu

IEEE/CVF International Conference on Computer Vision (ICCV), 2025

We present 7D Gaussian Splatting (7DGS), a unified framework representing scene elements as seven-dimensional Gaussians spanning position (3D), time (1D), and viewing direction (3D).

PolypSegTrack: Unified Foundation Model for Colonoscopy Video Analysis

Anwesa Choudhuri, Zhongpai Gao, Meng Zheng, Benjamin Planche, Terrence Chen, Ziyan Wu

Medical Image Computing and Computer Assisted Intervention (MICCAI), 2025

We introduce PolypSegTrack, a novel foundation model that jointly addresses polyp detection, segmentation, classification and unsupervised tracking in colonoscopic videos.

Seq2Time: Sequential Knowledge Transfer for Video LLM Temporal Grounding

Andong Deng, Zhongpai Gao, Anwesa Choudhuri, Benjamin Planche, Meng Zheng, Bin Wang, Chen Chen, Terrence Chen, Ziyan Wu

IEEE/CVF Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR), 2025

We propose Seq2Time, a data-oriented training paradigm that leverages sequences of images and short video clips to enhance temporal awareness in long videos.

6DGS: Enhanced Direction-Aware Gaussian Splatting for Volumetric Rendering

Zhongpai Gao, Benjamin Planche, Meng Zheng, Anwesa Choudhuri, Terrence Chen, Ziyan Wu

International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR), 2025

We propose 6DGS, an enhanced method for Gaussian Splatting that incorporates direction-aware features for improved volumetric rendering quality.
